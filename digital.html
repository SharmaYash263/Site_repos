Digital Art Pieces

This page highlights the evolution of my digital art journey, showcasing a timeline of my work as my skills and techniques have advanced. Each piece marks a significant milestone, illustrating the progression of my style and approach to digital art over time.

2022 | 2021 | 2020 | 2019 | 2018

2022

The Homelander

“The Homelander” was created on July 14, 2022, using my Apple iPad, Apple Pencil, and Autodesk Sketchbook drawing software. This stylized piece depicts Antony Starr in his role as “The Homelander” from the series The Boys. It was a massive project that required meticulous attention to texture, design, and poster layout. The custom superhero costume draws inspiration from the comic variant of the series. I’m particularly proud of this piece as it exceeded my expectations, especially in the texture of the suit, the detailed line art in the hair, and the nuanced shading around the face and hair.

7-Twelve

“7-Twelve” was created on May 16, 2022, using my Apple iPad, Apple Pencil, and Autodesk Sketchbook drawing software. In this digital piece, I depicted a dark blue 1991 Acura NSX parked in front of a parody gas station named “7-Twelve”. The inspiration came from social media posts of custom cars at gas stations. This project was a refreshing change of pace for me, as I was able to create something with a definite form, meticulously following the rules of perspective to ensure the building and vehicle looked accurate.

2021

Orange in Catalogue

“Orange in Catalogue” was created on July 27, 2021, using an Apple iPad, Apple Pencil, and Autodesk Sketchbook drawing software. This digital piece features a stylized person sitting against an orange background, dressed in pale-colored clothes. The piece was made as an exploration of color and form, emphasizing and experimenting the contrast between the vibrant background and the subtle tones of the clothing, and this was a piece that I had made in in regard to it's simplicity and effectiveness.

Blue in Orange

“Blue in Orange” was created on June 22, 2021, using an Apple iPad, Apple Pencil, and the Autodesk Sketchbook drawing software. The primary goal of this piece was to become more familiar with the tools in Autodesk Sketchbook while focusing on a drawing that predominantly uses one or two colors. The dynamic shading in the clothing adds an intriguing element to this color study.

Bizarre Adventure

“Bizarre Adventure” created on April 2, 2021, is a digital piece created using the Apple iPad, Apple Pencil, and the drawing software “Autodesk Sketchbook”. This stylized piece features Josuke Kujo from JoJo’s Bizarre Adventure, a series renowned for its dynamic color palette. The piece captures the vibrant and bold aesthetic of the season, emphasizing the unique and striking visual style that the series is known for. The use of vivid colors and intricate details brings Josuke Kujo to life, showcasing my ability to blend anime influences with digital art techniques. This piece highlights my dedication to capturing the essence of the character while experimenting with color and style to create a visually compelling piece.

Oni Samurai

“Oni Samurai” created on March 20, 2021, is a digital piece created using the Apple iPad, Apple Pencil, and the drawing software “Autodesk Sketchbook". It was a commissioned piece, meticulously drafted, created, and sold to a buyer. The design captures the fierce and imposing presence of the oni, blending traditional Japanese elements with a modern digital art style. The vibrant colors and detailed textures emphasize the character’s menacing yet captivating appearance. This piece showcases my ability to bring a client’s vision to life while maintaining a high level of artistic quality and creativity.

Blooming Season

“Blooming Season” created on March 18, 2021, is a digital piece created using the Apple iPad, Apple Pencil, and the drawing software “Autodesk Sketchbook". This transition to a more mobile digital art equipment had allowed me to go onto work on pieces when outside my work area. . The project combines everyday elements with surreal imagery, creating a striking and thought-provoking piece. The use of vibrant colors and intricate details highlights the contrast between the mundane and the extraordinary. This piece emphasizes the importance of creativity and imagination in digital art, showcasing my ability to blend realism with fantasy.

2020

Galaxy Man 2

“Galaxy Man 2” created on July 4, 2020, is a digital piece using the free, open-source digital painting application Krita. This piece is a follow-up to the original “Galaxy Man Moniker” piece. Over time, I developed a clearer vision of how I wanted to create pieces involving this character, making it a recognizable element of my artistic portfolio. This piece adheres to the same principles as the original, featuring bold, stylized elements with bright, warm colors contrasting with a central figure. Emphasizing the importance of minute details, I took great care in rendering the folds and grooves of the clothing, ensuring a complete and cohesive piece

The Weeknd

“The Weeknd” created on May 6,2020, is a digital piece using the free, open-source digital painting application Krita. This piece was inspired by the release of The Weeknd’s “After Hours” album and its music videos. It began as a physical drawing, which was later digitized for color choices and materials. This design and name moniker later became a recurring theme in my work, leading to more pieces featuring this character. It was important to keep the project stylized while ensuring it still resembled The Weeknd.

2019

Galaxy Man Moniker

“Galaxy Man Moniker” created on September 19, 2019, is a digital piece using the free, open-source digital painting application Krita. This piece began as a spontaneous late-night drawing, depicting a galaxy emerging from a man. At the time, I used clothes I frequently wore as a reference. This design and name moniker later became a recurring theme in my work, leading to more pieces featuring this character. I also connected this character to my moniker, “Lord_Yoshi”. This piece highlights the essential details for a custom project, and the use of color theory enhances the contrast and overall impact of the image.

2018

Sorcerer Supreme

“Sorcerer Supreme” created on June 26, 2018, is a digital piece using the free, open-source digital painting application Krita, and the medium Wacom Intuos Art Pen and Touch Tablet. This realistic drawing captures the powerful and heroic presence of Benedict Cumberbatch’s Dr. Strange with an attempt at realistic color choices. This piece highlights the detailed features and textures, bringing the character to life. I became more comfortable with the Wacom tablet through this piece. It taught me the value of drawing from reference while also showing me areas for improvement.

Goku Rose

"Goku Rose” created on March 4, 2018, is a digital piece using the free, open-source digital painting application Krita, and the medium Wacom Intuos Art Pen and Touch Tablet. This digital drawing captures the vibrant and dynamic presence of Goku Rose with a stunning blend of colors. This piece was the first digital piece I created, allowing me to explore and understand some of the tools available, while also planting the seeds of inspiration for future digital pieces.

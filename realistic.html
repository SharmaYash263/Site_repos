My Artwork

This page offers a timeline of my journey in creating realistic portraits, highlighting the progression of my skills and techniques over time. Each piece represents a key moment in my development, showcasing how my approach to capturing detail and expression has evolved.

2020 | 2019 | 2018 | 2017 | Pre-2016

2020

Mac Miller

“Mac Miller” created on March 8, 2020, is a pencil-only. This lifelike drawing beautifully captures Mac Miller’s introspective and expressive presence with meticulous attention to detail. Inspired by my deep admiration for his music and the emotions it evokes, the precise strokes bring out the intricate features and textures of his face, especially highlighting his hand gently resting on his face. This piece taught me the importance of drawing from reference, emphasizing the need to focus on minute details. The precision and depth achieved with just a pencil demonstrate my commitment to detail.

David in Sketch

"David in Sketch" created on February 16, 2020, uses color pencils and pen. This realistic drawing captures the timeless beauty and intricate details of Michelangelo’s David with immaculate attention to detail. The drawing stemmed from my sudden fascination with the original statue and the myth behind it. The precise strokes highlight the detailed features and textures, bringing the sculpture to life in vibrant color. This piece taught me the value of drawing what is seen in reference, emphasizing the importance of paying attention to minute details. The precision and depth achieved with simple color pencils showcase my dedication to detail and my ability to convey the elegance and grandeur of this iconic masterpiece. This piece would later inspire "David in Pop".

Kurt Cobain

"Kurt Cobain" created on January 6, 2020, using only pencil, is a realistic drawing that captures the iconic and introspective presence of the musician with immaculate attention to detail. The play of light and shadow emphasizes the depth and contours of his features, bringing him to life. This piece taught me the value of drawing what is seen in reference, emphasizing the importance of paying attention to minute details. The precision and depth achieved with a simple pencil highlight my commitment to realism.

2019

A Freind from Work

“A Freind from Work” created on July 13, 2019, uses only pencil. This realistic drawing captures the powerful and heroic presence of Chris Hemsworth’s Thor with immaculate attention to detail. This piece highlights the detailed features and textures, bringing the character to life. This piece taught me the value of drawing what is seen in reference, emphasizing the importance of paying attention to minute and tiny details. The precision and depth achieved with a simple pencil showcase my dedication to detail and my ability to convey emotion and intensity through realistic drawing techniques.

2018

Venom

"Venom" created on November 19,2018, uses only a ballpoint pen. This realistic drawing captures the intense and menacing presence of Tom Hardy's Venom with immaculate attention to detail. The intricate pen strokes highlight the detailed features and textures, bringing the character to life. This piece taught me the usefulness of everyday materials, such as the Bic pen, in creating highly detailed and impactful artwork.

Hatched Woman

“Hatched Woman” created on February 8, 2018, employs a mix of watercolor, marker, pencil, and pen. This cross-hatched drawing captures the powerful essence of a woman against the backdrop of a light blue sky, evoking a sense of calmness and mystique. The intricate cross-hatching technique highlights the detailed features and expressions, bringing the subject to life. The blending of cool colors in the background creates a calm and serene environment, enhancing the overall impact of the piece. This artwork showcases my dedication to detail and my ability to convey emotion through texture and color.

2017

Realistic Pug

“Realistic Pug” was created on May 23,2017, using only pencil. By sticking to one medium, it resulted in in a highly accurate drawing of a pug. This piece was my first commission, and I’m particularly proud of the level of detail and realism achieved using a day-to-day object. The meticulous attention to the the expressive eyes and the overall lifelike quality.

Pre-2016

Imperial Dragon

“Imperial Dragon” created on August 16, 2016, uses only pencil. This realistic sketch was one of my earliest attempts at realism. It features precise lines and intricate details, capturing the majestic presence of the dragon. The attention to texture and shading brings the dragon to life, highlighting its scales and powerful form.
